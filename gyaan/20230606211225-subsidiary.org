#+title:Subsidiary
#+filetags: :FINANCE:

- A subsidiary is a company that is controlled by another company, referred to as the parent company or the holding company.
- The controlling entity, usually owning more than 50% of the subsidiary's voting shares, has the ability to exercise control over the subsidiary's financial and operating policies.
- The parent company consolidates (combines) the financial results of its subsidiaries into its own financial statements.
- Subsidiaries can be wholly owned (100% ownership) or partially owned by the parent company.
